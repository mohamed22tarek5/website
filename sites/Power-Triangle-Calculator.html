Power Triangle Theory

  • Real Power (P): Measured in Watts (W), represents useful work done by the circuit
  • Reactive Power (Q): Measured in Volt-Amps Reactive (VAR), represents energy stored and returned by inductors and capacitors
  • Apparent Power (S): Measured in Volt-Amps (VA), the vector sum of real and reactive power
  • Power Factor (PF): Ratio of real power to apparent power (P/S), ranges from 0 to 1
  • Phase Angle: Angle between voltage and current waveforms, derived from the power triangle

Practical Applications

  • Power Factor Correction: Add capacitors to reduce reactive power and improve efficiency
  • Transformer Sizing: Apparent power determines transformer capacity requirements
  • Energy Efficiency: Higher power factor reduces energy losses in transmission
  • Motor Loads: Inductive loads like motors create lagging power factor
  • Capacitor Banks: Used to improve power factor in industrial settings
