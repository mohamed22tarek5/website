Overview

About the Project

Smart solar energy system integrating power electronics, energy storage, and IoT monitoring.

Problem Statement

Growing energy demands and reliance on fossil fuels have led to environmental degradation and energy insecurity. There's a critical need for efficient, scalable renewable energy solutions with real-time monitoring capabilities. The global energy crisis demands innovative solutions that combine sustainability with smart technology integration.

Project Objectives

Design and implement a smart solar energy system with maximum power point tracking (MPPT), efficient power conversion, battery storage, and IoT-based remote monitoring for optimal performance and reliability. Achieving 90%+ system efficiency, real-time monitoring, scalable design, and user-friendly dashboard interface.

Why Renewable Energy

Renewable energy sources are sustainable, environmentally friendly, and essential for reducing carbon emissions. They offer energy independence and long-term economic benefits compared to finite fossil fuels. Transitioning to renewables is a fundamental step toward global climate goals.

Why Solar Energy

Solar energy is abundant, scalable, and has rapidly decreasing implementation costs. The Middle East region receives some of the highest solar irradiation levels globally — an ideal environment for photovoltaic systems offering maximum energy yield year-round.

Architecture

System Overview

Solar PV Panel

Converts sunlight into DC electricity using monocrystalline silicon photovoltaic cells with high quantum efficiency.

  • Power Output: 200W rated
  • Cell Efficiency: 22%
  • MPPT Integration enabled
  • Voc = 24.3V, Isc = 8.4A

Buck Converter — MPPT Charger

Regulates PV voltage for optimal battery charging using the Perturb & Observe MPPT algorithm with 20kHz switching.

  • Converter Efficiency: 95%
  • MPPT Accuracy: 98.5%
  • Overcharge Protection included
  • Switching Freq: 20 kHz

Battery Storage System

Deep cycle lead-acid battery with integrated Battery Management System for safe, reliable energy storage.

  • Capacity: 100 Ah
  • Nominal Voltage: 12V DC
  • BMS Integration active
  • DoD limit: 50% max

Boost Converter

Steps up 12V battery voltage to 170V DC peak for inverter input with continuous conduction mode operation.

  • Input: 12V DC
  • Output: 170V DC peak
  • Efficiency: 93%
  • CCM operation

Pure Sine Wave Inverter

Full-bridge topology with SPWM modulation converts DC to clean AC power suitable for all household appliances.

  • Output: 220V AC, 50Hz
  • Rated Power: 500W
  • THD: <3% (IEEE 519)
  • Efficiency: 94.2%

IoT Monitoring — ESP32

Real-time monitoring via ESP32 microcontroller with WiFi, pushing live sensor data to Firebase cloud every second.

  • ESP32 dual-core MCU
  • Firebase Realtime DB
  • Sampling: 1 Hz continuous
  • Web dashboard accessible

Engineering

Technical Modules

Photovoltaic Modeling — One-Diode Model

I = Iph − I0[exp((V+IRs)/(nVt))−1] − (V+IRs)/Rsh

Mathematical representation of solar cell behavior using the single-diode equivalent circuit. Accounts for series resistance, shunt resistance, and diode ideality factor for accurate system modeling under variable irradiance and temperature conditions.

Buck Converter & MPPT Charger

Vout = D × Vin
D = ton / (ton + toff)

Step-down DC-DC converter uses Perturb & Observe MPPT algorithm. The duty cycle D is dynamically adjusted every switching period to track the maximum power point with 98.5% accuracy across all irradiance conditions.

Boost Converter Design

Vout = Vin / (1 − D)
ΔIL = (Vin × D) / (fs × L)

Boosts 12V battery voltage to 170V DC for inverter input. Operates in continuous conduction mode (CCM) achieving 93% efficiency. Inductor and capacitor values are optimized to minimize ripple below 1%.

Pure Sine Wave Inverter

fswitching = 20–50 kHz
THD < 3% (IEEE 519)

Full H-bridge inverter with Sinusoidal Pulse Width Modulation (SPWM) at 20kHz switching frequency. LC output filter eliminates harmonics. Achieves 220V RMS ± 1%, 50Hz ± 0.1Hz output meeting IEEE 519 power quality standards.

IoT Monitoring System

Sample Rate = 1 Hz continuous
Firebase Realtime DB sync

ESP32 reads voltage, current, and temperature sensors via ADC at 1Hz. Data is transmitted over WiFi to Firebase. The web dashboard renders live charts and alerts. Sensor accuracy is 99.5% with calibration against precision references.
